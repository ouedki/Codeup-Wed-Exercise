Yellowstone National Park FAQ

1. Is Yellowstone the largest national park?
No. More than half of Alaska’s national park units are larger, including Wrangell–St. Elias National Park and Preserve, which is the largest unit (13 million acres) in the National Park System.
2. Is Yellowstone the most visited national park?
Yellowstone is in the top five national parks for number of recreational visitors.
3. What is the difference between a national park and a national forest?
National parks are administered by the Department of the Interior and national forests by the Department of Agriculture.
4. How many rangers work in Yellowstone?
Approximately 773 people work for the National Park Service during the peak summer season.
5. How do you become a park ranger?
Park rangers have a variety of different duties. Most have a bachelor’s degree in a related field.
6.Can we swim in rivers and lakes?
Swimming is not recommended, and is occasionally prohibited, because most lakes and streams are dangerously cold.
7. What is the highest peak in the park?
Eagle Peak in the southeastern part of Yellowstone is the highest at 11,358 feet.
8. Why is Yellowstone called a biosphere reserve and a world heritage site?
The United Nations designated Yellowstone National Park as a biosphere reserve and a world heritage site in recognition of the worldwide significance of its natural and cultural resources.
9. What is the Continental Divide?
Think of the Continental Divide as the crest of the continent.
10 .Is Yellowstone open in winter?
Yes, though not all roads are open to cars.
